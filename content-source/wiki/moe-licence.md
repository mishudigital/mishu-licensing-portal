## Summary

An MOE licence is the registration a private education institution (Institut Pendidikan Swasta, IPS) needs to operate in Malaysia, granted by the Ministry of Education's Private Education Division and processed through the state JPN or district PPD under the Education Act 1996. The label covers several distinct centre types — tuition centre, enrichment centre, language centre, training centre, and kindergarten (tadika) — and the type drives almost everything: the company structure, the paid-up capital, the name format, who can sit on the board, and which students can enrol. The pre-screening job is to pin down which centre type the client actually wants, because a tuition centre and a language centre look similar to a layperson but have very different capital and ownership rules. A recurring practical hurdle across all types is converting the premises from commercial to education use, which needs an architect and council/Bomba approval.

## Body

### What the MOE licence is

Private education in Malaysia runs under the Education Act 1996. A centre registers as an Institut Pendidikan Swasta (IPS) with the Ministry of Education (KPM) through the Bahagian Pendidikan Swasta, handled on the ground by the state education department (JPN) or district education office (PPD). The registration is centre-specific: the company must show that education is one of its registered activities, name the centre to a prescribed format, meet a paid-up capital floor, and appoint a governance structure (chairman, board of governors, principal, teachers) before the premises is inspected and the licence issued.

### The centre types at a glance

The single most useful tool for scoping is the type comparison. The four main non-kindergarten types differ as follows:

- **Tuition centre (Pusat Tuisyen)** — teaches the local school syllabus only (KSSR / KSSM), students aged 7 to 17. Can be an enterprise, PLT, or Sdn Bhd. Name must start with "PUSAT TUISYEN". Paid-up capital RM10,000. Board of governors of 3 (local only). The lightest-weight type.
- **Enrichment centre (Pusat Perkembangan Minda)** — cognitive-development programmes using specific methodologies (tools, musical instruments, calculation or writing techniques); supplementary to formal schooling, and the certificate is for attendance only. Students aged 7 and above. Sdn Bhd only. Name must start with "PUSAT PERKEMBANGAN MINDA". Paid-up capital RM100,000 (KL) or RM50,000 (Selangor). Board of 5 (local only). The SSM company name must itself signal education (e.g. Smart, Learning, Education).
- **Language centre (Pusat Bahasa)** — languages only (e.g. Japanese, French, English), students aged 7 and above. Sdn Bhd only. Name must start with "PUSAT BAHASA". Paid-up capital RM100,000. Board of 5 — and uniquely, foreigners are allowed on the board and as teachers. The premises must be a commercial unit with a layout plan approved by PBT and Bomba (needs an architect).
- **Training centre (Pusat Latihan)** — technical fields (IT, business, accounting and similar); short certificate courses not exceeding 3 months or 20 credit hours per level. Malaysian students 16 and above; international students 18 to 45. Sdn Bhd only. Name must start with "PUSAT LATIHAN". Paid-up capital RM50,000. Board of 5 (local only).

Across all four: the shareholding rule is 30% Bumiputera / 70% local or foreign; the chairman is one local person who cannot be a board member; the principal is one local person who cannot be a board member; and a minimum of one teacher is required (local only, except the language centre where a foreigner is allowed).

### Foreign equity by nationality

Foreign ownership in education is governed by each country's Free Trade Agreement with Malaysia, so the cap varies by the investor's nationality. The worked figures captured for the language centre are: Spain, India, and the United States up to 20%; China, Japan, and Korea up to 49%; Singapore and Indonesia up to 51%. Confirm the current cap for the client's nationality before advising, and note this links to immigration work — a language centre is the one type that can place foreigners on the board and in teaching roles, which feeds Employment Pass planning.

### Kindergarten (Tadika) — its own rules

A private kindergarten is a separate scheme under the Private Education Division, set out in the 2021 Garis Panduan Penubuhan Tadika Swasta. Key points:

- **Ownership:** must be owned and operated by Malaysian citizens; if incorporated under ROC, minimum 30% Bumiputera equity.
- **Premises:** allowed in government-office premises, single or multi-storey terrace/bungalow houses, or dedicated kindergarten space — subject to the multi-storey building guideline (up to about level 5 / 18 metres) and PBT approval. A kindergarten in a residential area needs a written permission letter from the residents' association / neighbourhood. Tadika (kindergarten) and Taska (nursery) may not operate at the same address, though they may sit in adjacent premises.
- **Principal:** must hold a diploma in Early Childhood Education or equivalent qualification/experience.
- **Management:** at least two persons (ahli pengelola).
- **Operations:** maximum learning time 4 hours, ending by about 5:30 p.m.; maximum fees not exceeding RM150.
- **Process and timeline:** name checked with PPD and SSM first; online registration plus two printed ring-file sets submitted in person to the PPD; premises inspection; presentation to a review panel; licence issued. The PPD sets a small processing fee (e.g. RM15 for three years). The whole process takes roughly 6 to 9 months.

### Common documents (tuition centre checklist as the model)

The application checklists share a common spine. For a tuition centre the Private Education Division asks for:

- The establishment form (Borang BPS-1) and a covering application letter.
- A certified copy of the applicant's IC.
- SSM documents: for an Sdn Bhd, the latest Section 14 and Section 51, the registration certificate, particulars/summary of share capital, summary of shareholders, particulars of directors, and the M&A or practice directive stating education as a company activity. For a sole proprietor/partnership/PLT, the business-name approval (PNA.42). For a society/association/foundation/cooperative, the ROS registration and constitution showing education as an activity.
- Proof of financial position: bank statement in the centre's name showing the required paid-up capital (RM10,000 for a tuition centre, with 30% Bumiputera equity for an Sdn Bhd).
- Governance appointments — chairman of the board of governors, board members, principal, and teachers — each with appointment letter, IC, and academic qualifications.

### The premises conversion hurdle

A point worth raising early with any MOE client: the premises usually needs its status converting from commercial to education use, which runs through the town planning department (Jabatan Perancang Bandar) and the building department (Jabatan Bangunan) and requires an architect to prepare and submit the layout plan (and Bomba approval). This is a separate cost and timeline from the licence itself.

### MISHU service fee

On the worked quotation, MISHU charged RM7,000 (excl. SST) for the MOE licence service — paperwork, processing, and checking with the Private Education Division. The architectural work to convert commercial premises to education use is quoted separately at RM5,000–RM10,000, set by the architect, covering the town planning and building department submissions. JAKIM-style agency fees and disbursements are borne by the client.
