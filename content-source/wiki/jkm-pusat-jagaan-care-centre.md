# JKM Pusat Jagaan Licence (care centres — older children, disabled, elderly, women)

**Status:** emerging
**Last updated:** 2026-06-22
**Sources:** `RAW/JKM License/Daycare Centre [JKM]/JKM SELANGOR CHECKLIST 2025/JKM Selangor.pdf`

## Summary

A Pusat Jagaan is a care centre registered with the Department of Social Welfare (JKM) under the Care Centres Act 1993, distinct from the TASKA childcare scheme for children under four. It covers day or residential care for older children and teenagers (four and above), persons with disabilities (OKU), the elderly, and women, run by a private operator or an NGO. The single source compiled so far is the Selangor (PKMD Klang) registration document pack, which sets out the application and records checklists, the caregiver-to-resident ratios by category, and the floor-space calculation. Treat this as the Selangor baseline pending the national Care Centres Act guideline.

## Body

### What it covers and how it differs from TASKA

The Selangor document pack registers a "Pusat Jagaan" by category: children (kanak-kanak), persons with disabilities (OKU), the elderly or aged (warga tua / warga emas), women (wanita), or mixed (pelbagai). Crucially, the ratio table states that children under four are not registered here — they are registered under the TASKA Act 1984 instead. So the line between the two JKM regimes is age and client group: under-fours go to TASKA; children aged four and above, the disabled, the elderly, and women go to Pusat Jagaan. A centre may be daily (harian) or residential (berkediaman), and management may be private (swasta) or NGO.

### Caregiver-to-resident ratios

The required minimum ratios in the Selangor pack are:

| Category | Residents : caregiver |
|----------|----------------------|
| Children under 4 | Register under TASKA Act 1984 (not here) |
| Children / teens 4 and above | 18 : 1 |
| Persons with disabilities (OKU) | 10 : 1 |
| Severely disabled / bedridden OKU | 4 : 1 |
| Elderly, independent | 18 : 1 |
| Elderly, bedridden / ill | 4 : 1 |
| Women / adult men | 18 : 1 |

### Floor-space calculation

Floor space is allocated per resident and varies by category: 3.5 square metres for children under 10, persons with disabilities, and bedridden elderly; 3.0 square metres for teenagers/adults and (independent) elderly. The maximum number of residents is the total activity floor area divided by the per-resident rate. The worked example in the pack: 145.75 square metres ÷ 3.5 = 41 children maximum, or ÷ 3.0 = 48 elderly maximum.

### Records to keep on site

The pack's records checklist (Bahagian B) requires staff personal files (personal details, proof of qualifications, skills, work experience, appointment letter), plus health, salary/allowance, duty-allocation, EPF and SOCSO contribution records; resident personal files (background, family/next-of-kin, problems and progress, birth certificate or IC, OKU card if any), plus a resident register, attendance records, property/savings records for residential centres, health records, and schooling records; and management records (visit/agency log, fire-drill and safety-training records, activity and incident log book, committee records, donation records). The centre must also display a signboard meeting the local authority's specification and display the Certificate of Registration, and keep the first-aid kit and fire extinguishers in working order.

### Welfare and safety conditions

The implementation checklist covers fire prevention (inspection by JBPM, building-evacuation and fire-drill practice with records kept, clear unobstructed exits, maintained working extinguishers) and health and welfare (toilets at a ratio of 1 per 5 residents, kept clean; sufficient water; separate sleeping, laundry, and bathing areas for male and female residents in residential centres; separate sleeping arrangements for married couples; clean, nutritious and sufficient food on an approved diet scale with the menu displayed; periodic medical checks and reporting of communicable disease; a sick bay; a maintained first-aid kit; and procedures for handling a death, including notifying next-of-kin). Food menus must be approved via the Ministry of Health nutrition portal (nutrition.moh.gov.my).

## Open Questions

- **Single-state, single-source.** Only the Selangor (PKMD Klang) pack is compiled. Obtain the national Care Centres Act 1993 guideline and a second state's pack to confirm the ratios, floor-space rates, fees, and certificate validity apply nationally, and to promote this article from emerging to established.
- **No fee, timeline, or certificate validity captured.** The Selangor pack is a document/records checklist; it does not state the registration fee, processing time, or how long the certificate lasts. Confirm these from the Act guideline.
- **Operator qualification and technical-agency support.** The TASKA scheme requires a KAAK/KAP-certified operator and JBPM/health/PBT support; confirm the equivalent operator-qualification and agency-approval requirements for Pusat Jagaan.
- **No MISHU service fee on record.** No MISHU quotation for Pusat Jagaan registration was found in RAW.
