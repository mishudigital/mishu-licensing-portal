# MBPJ composite requirements (Petaling Jaya)

**Status:** emerging
**Last updated:** 2026-06-21
**Sources:** `RAW/Forms/Majlis Bandaraya Petaling Jaya/Panduan_permohonan_lesen_komposit_premis_dan_iklan_kemaskini MBPJ.pdf`, `RAW/Forms/Majlis Bandaraya Petaling Jaya/Notes for MBPJ.docx`

## Summary

Majlis Bandaraya Petaling Jaya (MBPJ) runs its composite premises-and-signboard licensing entirely through the eLesen online system at elesen.mbpj.gov.my, handled by the Jabatan Pelesenan (Licensing Department). An application moves through five steps — register, select activity, upload documents, save the acknowledgement slip, then pay and print the licence — with feedback promised within 24 working hours. The general document set mirrors the standard composite list, and every signboard illustration must clear Dewan Bahasa dan Pustaka (DBP) before submission. The licensing year ends on 31 December, so the renewal cycle and cancellations are tied to that date.

## Body

### Who can apply and what to prepare first

The online applicant must be a director, business owner, or company secretary registered with SSM, or a chair, secretary, or committee member registered with a relevant body such as the Registrar of Societies (ROS). Since October 2020 a company representative can also apply, provided an authorisation letter (surat penurunan kuasa) in MBPJ's format is attached.

Before first registration, have ready: a copy of the IC or passport (pdf/jpg), the SSM or business/professional-body registration document (pdf/jpg), a working email address, a mobile number that can receive SMS, a residential address, and — where the applicant is not a company director — the authorisation letter (pdf/png).

### The five steps

1. Register as a user on the eLesen system.
2. Search for the main activity, select or add the company name, and add further activities if the business runs more than one.
3. Upload documents. Each file is pdf up to 5MB, or jpg/png up to 2MB; multiple files can be uploaded at once. (The FAQ separately states a 2MB upload limit, so keep files small and split large ones.)
4. Generate and save the acknowledgement slip (slip akuan penerimaan), which carries the application serial number (EL-xxxxxxxxxxx) and a QR code for status tracking. The slip is not a licence and cannot be displayed at the business premises.
5. Once approved, pay and print the licence online.

### General documents required

The general document set for any activity is: a copy of the MyKad or passport; the key pages of the stamped tenancy or sale-and-purchase agreement; a floor-plan sketch with area measurements; a colour photo showing the activity inside the premises; a colour photo of the exterior with the adjacent premises; the SSM business or company registration certificate; the SSM owner or director information; and DBP confirmation for each advertisement illustration. DBP approval is obtained online first through dbpsahbahasa.my. Specific activities carry their own additional document requirements — MBPJ lists separate sheets for trade/business/industrial, food establishments, beauty and health-care centres, private parking, hotels, cyber centres, entertainment centres, and premises advertising.

### Payment, deposit and stickers

All licence payments and licence printing can be done online. Where an application carries a deposit (cagaran), the deposit bill is settled online via epay.mbpj.gov.my using the deposit account number (format Dxxxxxxxxxxxxx) found in the licence status menu. A premise sticker (pelekat) is issued by post or at the counter only for accounts carrying the RM25 administration charge; it is issued once and must be displayed permanently outside the premises.

Five licence types cannot be printed online and are handled separately: entertainment, cyber centre, hawker/night-market/Smart Trak, health-care centre, and outdoor (billboard) advertising.

### Application statuses

The portal shows the application as DRAF (being filled, editable), BARU (submitted), PROSES (received and under officer review, not editable), TIDAK LENGKAP (returned for missing documents), PRA KELULUSAN (approved pending site inspection — payment and printing of a temporary licence/acknowledgement card allowed), LULUS (inspected and passed at committee — permanent licence can be printed), TANGGUH (deferred for a stated reason, e.g. an unpaid parking or deposit bill), or BATAL (cancelled).

### Team-logged nuances

All MBPJ licence applications expire on 31 December, so the licence period runs to the year end regardless of when it was granted. To cancel a licence, draft an email to smartlesen@mbpj.gov.my. Cancellation of a BARU, PROSES, or PRA KELULUSAN application also has to be requested by email; only DRAF and TIDAK LENGKAP applications can be cancelled by the applicant directly. DBP signboard approval is valid for one year only.
