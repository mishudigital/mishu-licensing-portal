## Summary

A MOTAC licence lets a company run a tourism business in Malaysia under the Tourism Industry Act 1992 [Act 482], administered by the Ministry of Tourism, Arts and Culture and applied for through the TOURLIST portal. The main licence is TOBTAB — Tour Operating Business and Travel Agency Business — which splits into domestic (inbound) tour operating, outbound tour operating, and travel agency (ticketing), with an Umrah/Ziarah add-on. The decisive factor for scoping, especially for foreign clients, is the equity-and-capital table: a wholly Malaysian-owned travel agency needs RM200,000 paid-up, but a foreign-owned tour operator can need up to RM1.5 million, and outbound tour operating is closed to foreign companies entirely. Tourist accommodation (hotels) is a separate MOTAC registration, classified into City, Resort, Highland, Boutique, and Innovative. The pre-screening job is to fix the exact licence sub-type and the shareholders' nationality, then read the paid-up capital straight off Appendix A.

## Body

### What the MOTAC licence is

Tourism businesses are licensed under the Tourism Industry Act 1992 [Act 482]. The applicant must be a private limited company (Sdn Bhd) incorporated under the Companies Act 2016, with "Nature of Business" in its SSM e-Info stating tour operating and/or travel agency, and a company name that reflects tourism or travel. Applications run entirely through the TOURLIST system (www.tourlist.gov.my). The current guideline is effective from 1 August 2024.

### The TOBTAB licence types

- **Domestic tour operating (Pengendalian Pelancongan Dalam Negeri)** — inbound tours within Malaysia.
- **Outbound tour operating (Pengendalian Pelancongan Luar Negeri)** — tours outside Malaysia. An Umrah/Ziarah scope can be added.
- **Travel agency (Agensi Pengembaraan)** — ticketing and travel agency services.

These can be held singly or combined. MISHU's internal framing lists the practical types as inbound, outbound, ticketing, and travel accommodation.

### Equity and paid-up capital — read this off Appendix A

This is the heart of the scoping decision. The required paid-up capital depends on the licence type, whether the shareholder is local or foreign, the foreign shareholder's origin (ASEAN vs non-ASEAN), and whether the premises is urban (Majlis Bandaraya/Perbandaran area) or rural (Majlis Daerah area). The key cases:

Foreign-owned companies:

- Domestic tour operating, foreign company (ASEAN or non-ASEAN): up to 100% foreign equity allowed, but paid-up capital RM1,500,000.
- Domestic tour operating + travel agency, ASEAN foreign company: 70% equity (Singapore/Cambodia only) or 51% (other than Singapore/Cambodia); paid-up RM1,000,000.
- Domestic tour operating + travel agency, non-ASEAN foreign company: 30% equity; paid-up RM500,000.
- Outbound tour operating, any foreign company: **not allowed**.
- A foreign company holding more than 51% equity cannot hold the licence, except the wholly-foreign domestic-only and 70% Singapore/Cambodia cases above.

Wholly Malaysian-owned (citizen or local company, 100%):

- Domestic tour operating: RM200,000 (urban) / RM50,000 (rural).
- Outbound tour operating: RM200,000.
- Travel agency: RM200,000 (urban) / RM100,000 (rural).
- Domestic + outbound: RM200,000.
- Domestic + travel agency: RM200,000 (urban) / RM150,000 (rural).
- Outbound + travel agency: RM200,000.
- Domestic + outbound + travel agency: RM200,000.

A reduced RM50,000 paid-up applies for domestic-only tour operating in Labuan, Langkawi, Perlis, and the Pahang resort islands, but those companies may not open branches elsewhere.

Two further conditions matter for foreign clients: foreign shareholders must have at least three years of tourism experience, and shareholders must not hold equity in any other tourism company licensed under Act 482. MISHU's internal rule of thumb for a clean application is a local shareholder holding 100%, paid-up capital around RM250,000, and — where foreign participation is needed — only foreign corporate shareholders, at a permitted percentage with the correspondingly higher paid-up capital.

### Required documents

- SSM e-Info, printed no more than 14 days before the application.
- MyKad (Malaysian) or passport (non-Malaysian) for all directors and shareholders; employment pass for foreign directors.
- For a foreign company shareholder: a certified copy of the company registration certificate, certified by Tourism Malaysia, the Malaysian Embassy, or the High Commission in the relevant country.
- Director's Letter of Undertaking (Form BA/2).
- PERKESO (SOCSO) registration confirmation.
- Banking information (front page of company account statements) and a director's resolution for each company account.
- For an outbound licence: a letter of cooperation with foreign travel operators and a draft travel-package proposal.
- A premises to operate from.

### Procedure, premises, and TTMC

Apply through TOURLIST with all documents uploaded. Conditional approval is targeted within 5 working days. After conditional approval the company has 6 months to set up premises: minimum 100 sq ft, proper structure, in a commercial zone, supported by a tenancy agreement or S&P, floor plan, photos, and signage. The premises is inspected within 30 working days. At least one company representative must attend the TTMC course and the company must hold a valid TTMC certificate. The licence fee is paid after approval and the licence is printed online. A rejected application can be appealed once, within 14 days.

### Fees and validity

- Processing fee: RM100 per application via TOURLIST.
- Annual licence fee (effective 1 August 2024), main / additional: domestic tour operation RM250 / RM150; outbound tour operation RM500 / RM350; outbound (Umrah) RM500 / RM350; travel agency RM200 / RM100.
- Validity: 1 year. MISHU's internal estimate for the end-to-end process is 3 to 4 months.

### Team-logged nuance — foreign directors and bank-account control

A real MISHU case sharpened two points that the guideline only implies:

- **A foreign director must hold a valid Employment Pass to stay on the board for the application.** On a fully foreign-owned company with one foreign and one local Malaysian director, the foreign director did not yet have an Employment Pass at the time of the MOTAC application. MOTAC would not allow it: the foreign director had to resign, leaving only the local director, for the application to proceed. So before applying, check each foreign director's EP status — no EP means either secure it first or have the foreign director step down and rely on a local director.
- **Bank-account control must sit with the local director.** MOTAC scrutinises the board resolution governing the company bank account, which had to show the local Malaysian director as the controlling signatory. A foreigner cannot be the person in control of the account for the purpose of the application.

Both points reinforce why this licence is tied to immigration work: getting the foreign director's Employment Pass in place first is often the cleaner path than reshuffling the board.

### MISHU service fee

On a worked Klang Valley quotation for an inbound travel agency, MISHU's service fee for the MOTAC licence was RM5,000 (excluding the MOTAC licence fees, which the client bears), bundled with the composite premises licence (RM850) for a total service fee of RM5,850. The government processing fee (RM100), the annual licence fee, and the paid-up-capital requirement are separate.

### Tourist accommodation (PPP) — a separate registration

Hotels and tourist accommodation are registered separately by the Commissioner under section 31C(1) of Act 482, and classified into five categories: City Hotel, Resort Hotel (island/beach/lake/river), Highland Hotel (over 300 m above sea level), Boutique Hotel (unique, personal, luxurious concept), and Innovative Hotel (non-conventional structures — igloo, tree house, container, capsule, and the like). A spa within a tourism setting has its own MOTAC classification guideline. These are distinct from the TOBTAB licence and would each warrant their own detailed pass.
