# MPS Selayang composite requirements

**Status:** emerging
**Last updated:** 2026-06-21
**Sources:** `RAW/Forms/Majlis Perbandaran Selayang/Senarai semak MPS.pdf`

## Summary

Majlis Perbandaran Selayang (MPS) issues a composite business-premises-and-signboard licence through its Licensing Department (Persiaran 3, Bandar Baru Selayang, Batu Caves). The MPS checklist runs to seventeen general items, then a long activity-specific table covering 36 business types, plus separate rule sets for vehicle workshops, signboards, and general premises conditions. Two MPS-specific points stand out: the general set asks for photos of a mounted fire extinguisher, a rubbish bin, and a first-aid box, and the signboard rule requires the national language to be 30% larger than any other language.

## Body

### General documents (17-item checklist)

The SSM portion accepts one of three forms: a Sdn Bhd set (Form 9, the MAA, Form 24, Form 49); an Enterprise or PLT set (Form D, business information, current owner information); or an association/society/club registration certificate. On top of that: a floor plan; a location plan; the tenancy agreement or land grant; an IC or passport copy; a colour advertisement visual approved by DBP; a photo of the advertisement in position; the fire-extinguisher purchase receipt; a photo of the fire extinguisher hung one metre from the floor; the door-tax (cukai pintu) bill or confirmation from the MPS Valuation Department; a photo of the rubbish bin; a photo of the first-aid box; the CF copy or confirmation from the MPS Building Department; an official letter authorising a representative where one is used; and, if no signboard is being applied for, a letter explaining why none is needed.

### Activity-specific additions

The MPS table lists extra documents for 36 activities. Food premises (restaurant, café, food processing and packaging) need the owner's and assistants' typhoid injection, food-handling course certificate, a pest-control contractor appointment, FoSIM registration with the Ministry of Health, and a photo of the installed grease trap. Other notable cases: a money changer needs a Bank Negara licence; a moneylender and a pawnshop need KPKT licences; a foreign-owned hypermarket/supermarket/departmental/specialty store needs KPDNKK distributive-trade approval plus Town Planning approval; used-goods sale needs a Police licence; education-type centres (kindergarten, private school, tuition, language, computer, vocational, skills) need State Education Department approval; a childcare centre needs JKM registration; printing (except inkjet) needs a KDN printing-machine licence; in-building parking needs Town Planning approval, the CCC/CF, and CCTV photos; a hotel needs Town Planning, the CCC/CF, BOMBA approval and pest control; chemical, toxic, flammable, hazardous or scheduled-waste stores need JAS, BOMBA and JKKP approvals; a pharmacy needs Pharmacy Board registration; CD/DVD sale needs FINAS approval; a clinic needs an annual practising certificate; professionals (architect, engineer, lawyer, quantity surveyor, land surveyor, planner) need registration with their professional body; health/beauty centres need typhoid plus a health examination; massage and reflexology add a blood test (HIV, hepatitis, VDRL) and a Police/BOMBA technical review; a cyber café needs pest control, typhoid, and a Police/BOMBA review; a quarry needs Town Planning plus a Forestry or Land Office permit; an entertainment centre needs a Police/BOMBA technical review and pest control; fuel and explosives storage needs BOMBA and JKKP; and a laundry needs Suruhanjaya Tenaga and BOMBA approval.

### Vehicle workshops (by building type)

MPS sets conditions for vehicle repair, servicing, tyre and accessory shops by premises type. A shop lot must rent two parking bays in front of the premises (three for a corner lot) and trade only from 8am to 7pm. A shop office is allowed only on a corner lot, must rent two bays, and trades 8am to 7pm. An industrial-area workshop trades 8am to 8pm, must carry public-liability insurance, and must run weekly pest control with the appointment certificate or receipt shown on inspection. A land lot trades 8am to 8pm, needs neighbour support within a 200-metre radius, must be free of public complaints, and follows the same weekly pest-control rule.

### Signboard rules

A sign or signboard may not be installed before the business licence is obtained. The applicant submits a colour sketch in the set format showing the board size in metres, the activity in the national language, the MPS/licence account number at the bottom right, and whether the sign is lit or unlit; hand-drawn and black-and-white illustrations are not accepted. Projection beyond the shop-lot boundary is capped at 600mm where the sign is at least 2.75m above the road and 1200mm where it is at least 5.00m above the road. A lit board may use only non-blinking lights. The national language must be 30% larger than any other language, given priority in position and grammar, and confirmed by DBP first (dbpsahbahasa.my).

### General premises conditions

Standing conditions cover safety (a complete first-aid box; suitable fire extinguishers approved by BOMBA; adequate lighting and ventilation; well-kept store, stairs and emergency doors; no obstruction of public areas without MPS permission), cleanliness (proper water, electricity and sewerage; "No Smoking" and "Keep Clean" signs displayed; premises kept pest-free; clean toilets; sewage routed to the sewerage system), and waste (a sufficient 240L MBG bin, plastic bags mandatory). The licence cannot be transferred without approval, must be displayed at all times, and can be cancelled by MPS without notice; holders are encouraged to display the royal portraits.
