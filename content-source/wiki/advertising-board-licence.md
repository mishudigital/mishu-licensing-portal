# Advertising Board Licence (standalone billboard / LED)

**Status:** emerging
**Last updated:** 2026-06-23
**Sources:** [[MISHU Business License Services - Advertising Board (DBKL)]]

## Summary

An advertising board licence covers a standalone outdoor advertising structure — a billboard, hoarding, or permanent LED display — as opposed to the shopfront signboard that comes bundled into a business's composite premises licence. It is granted by the local council; the DBKL case in RAW is for a permanent LED billboard. The licence is annual, and the council charges a recurring yearly fee on top of the one-off application work. MISHU's documented service fee for arranging one is RM3,000, exclusive of the council fees.

## Body

### What this licence is, and how it differs from a signboard

Every business with premises needs a signboard licence for the sign over its own door — that is the advertising component of the [[fnb-composite-licence]] and is handled inside the composite application (the council articles cover the DBP-approved signboard rules in detail). An advertising board licence is a separate thing: it authorises a dedicated advertising structure such as a permanent LED billboard, typically displaying third-party or large-format advertising rather than identifying the occupier's own shop. The MISHU DBKL quotation scopes exactly this — "Item: Permanent LED Billboard" — and lists it as its own line, distinct from the composite signboard.

### Granting authority and scope of work

The licence is issued by the local council; in the documented case that is DBKL (Kuala Lumpur). MISHU's scope of work for the application, per the quotation, is: preparing and processing the documents, obtaining the checking report from all relevant government departments at DBKL, and delivering the licence. The quotation pairs the advertising board licence with a composite licence application, which is consistent with the structure needing to sit against a licensed premises or applicant.

### Fees

Two layers of cost, kept separate in the quotation:

- MISHU service fee: RM3,000 (excludes SST and any fees payable to the local council).
- Council fee: estimated RM3,000–5,000 per year, payable to the local council, varying by location and size of the board. This is a recurring annual charge, borne by the client.

Licence fees set by the government agency are decided later by the agency and borne by the client — the quotation flags them as a disbursement, not part of the service fee.

### Sales-scoping note

This is a single-source quotation (DBKL, dated 26 July 2022), so treat the figures as indicative for scoping rather than fixed. The council fee in particular is explicitly location- and size-dependent, so for any prospect the actual yearly cost has to be confirmed with the council once the board's dimensions and placement are known.

## Open Questions

- The Malay-language verification requirement for advertisements (DBP / the root-level "TERMA DAN SYARAT PENGESAHAN BAHASA MELAYU DALAM IKLAN" document in RAW) almost certainly applies to billboard copy as well as shopfront signs — confirm and fold in on a future pass.
- No official council guideline for standalone advertising-structure licensing has been ingested yet (only the MISHU quotation). The DBKL advertising/billboard bylaw and fee schedule would let the article move from emerging to established and confirm the RM3,000–5,000/year band.
- Whether the advertising board licence can be held without an accompanying premises/composite licence, or must always sit against one, is not settled by the single source.
