# F&B composite licence

**Status:** emerging
**Last updated:** 2026-06-19
**Sources:** `RAW/Composite License Checklist.docx`, `RAW/F&B/QUOTATION/MISHU Business License Services - Restaurant(DBKL).docx`

## Summary

An F&B premises in Malaysia operates under a composite licence issued by the local council, which bundles the business premises licence with the signboard (advertising) licence. The base document set is broadly consistent across councils, but each council varies in the exact copies required, submission method (online portal vs over-the-counter), and supporting approvals. F&B specifically adds health and fire requirements on top of the standard composite set — most notably a food-handler typhoid (TY6) injection and a food-handling course for the applicant and all staff, plus grease-trap and premise food-department checks.

## Body

### What the composite licence covers

For a restaurant or food premises, the council composite licence combines two things into one application: the right to operate the business at the premises, and approval for the business signboard. The signboard wording must be approved by Dewan Bahasa dan Pustaka (DBP) for correct Malay grammar before the council will accept it — MISHU's business consultant handles the DBP application.

### Standard document set (common across councils)

Most councils ask for the same core documents, with only the number of copies and a few extras differing:

- Director's / owner's IC (or passport + visa for non-citizens)
- Passport-sized photographs of the applicant
- SSM company documents (Form 24 & Form 49, or Form A/B/D for sole proprietors and partnerships; MyData printout where required)
- Stamped tenancy agreement, sale-and-purchase agreement, or land grant / owner's permission
- Certificate of Fitness for Occupancy (CF) or Certificate of Completion and Compliance (CCC)
- Floor plan of the premises
- Location plan (Google Maps source is accepted)
- Current-year assessment tax (cukai taksiran / cukai pintu) bill
- Premises photographs — front, rear, interior, and side view if it is a corner lot
- Signboard visual with measurements, carrying DBP approval
- Fire-extinguisher receipt and a photo of the extinguisher mounted one metre from the floor (some councils)
- Temporary construction permit approval letter, if any partition or renovation has been done

### F&B-specific additions

On top of the standard set, food premises typically require:

- **Food-handler medical check including TY6 (typhoid) injection** for the applicant and all staff (explicitly required by MP Sepang; standard health-department practice for F&B).
- **Food-handling course certificate** for the applicant and all staff.
- **Health department / premise food department checks** — including grease-trap collection service.
- **Fire and Rescue Department (BOMBA)** support letter or inspection.

Where alcohol, live or recorded music, or halal certification apply, separate licences sit alongside the composite (see Related).

### Council variations observed

| Council | Submission | Notable specifics |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| DBKL (Kuala Lumpur) | Online via elesen.dbkl.gov.my | Account created with director's IC; ask client for existing account / password. Confirm office number, renovations, signboard lighting, business hours. |
| MBPJ (Petaling Jaya) | Online via elesen.mbpj.gov.my | DBP signboard approval valid one year only. Ask about signboard lighting and bank account details. |
| MBSJ (Subang Jaya) | Form-based | Copies must be crossed and marked "Urusan MBSJ sahaja". |
| MBSA (Shah Alam) | Over the counter | Prepare 4 copies; partition permit letter if applicable. |
| MP Kajang | Over the counter | Application form purchased at counter; needs real signature and company stamp; BOMBA letter collected from Hulu Langat branch. Process fee RM50 (business) / RM100 (industrial). |
| MP Sepang | Online and counter | Explicitly requires food-handler TY6 injection + food-handling course for applicant and all staff; entertainment form if any entertainment activity. |
| MP Selayang | Online (subject to portal availability) | Standard composite set; letter explaining why no signboard, if not applying for one. |

### Services and indicative fees (sales scoping reference)

A worked DBKL restaurant composite example breaks the engagement into these service lines (professional fees, exclusive of SST and agency/licence charges, which the client bears):

- Composite licence application — paperwork, processing, and checking report across government departments / local council
- DBP approval — RM850 (inclusive of printing and transport)
- Fire and Rescue Department — RM600
- Health department / premise food department (incl. grease-trap application) — RM600
- Indicative total MISHU service fee: RM2,050

This gives sales consultants a starting structure for an F&B fee proposal. Actual figures vary with case complexity and council, and licence fees set by each agency are borne by the client.
