## Summary

JAKIM halal certification is the Malaysian government's recognition that a product, premises, or service meets Islamic requirements across its whole supply and process chain. It is governed by the Manual Prosedur Pensijilan Halal Malaysia (Domestik) 2020 — the MPPHM — and the relevant Malaysian Standards (MS 1500:2019 for food, MS 2634:2019 for cosmetics, MS 2400 series for logistics, and others). MISHU does not certify directly; we refer clients to two halal consultancy partners (HQC and NAMI) who handle the documentation, training, and JAKIM audit. The consultant's job before referral is to confirm the client can actually meet the basic eligibility gate, then identify which of the nine certification schemes applies. This article is the pre-referral checklist: the universal requirements every applicant must satisfy, plus the category-specific requirements for the main schemes.

### The basic eligibility gate (check this first, every time)

Under the MPPHM, every applicant — regardless of scheme — must hold:

1. A valid business registration from SSM (Suruhanjaya Syarikat Malaysia) — or a cooperative registration, or an establishment document under an Act of Parliament / ministry where relevant. The HQC checklist asks for SSM Form 9 and Form 49.
2. A valid licence or approval from the local authority (PBT), or a valid supporting/confirmation letter from a government agency. This is the council business premises licence — see [[fnb-composite-licence]].
3. A valid Food Premises Registration from BKKM (the food-premises registration under FoSIM Domestik), where relevant — applies to F&B products and premises.

On top of the paperwork, the applicant must be able to meet the substantive halal conditions:

- **Muslim Malaysian staff.** Permanent Muslim Malaysian-citizen workers must be employed in the processing area throughout operating hours or each shift. The number scales with industry size: large and medium industry need a minimum of two, plus a Halal Executive at each branch and an Internal Halal Committee; small and micro industry need a minimum of one, with a Halal Supervisor (small) and an internal halal control system. (For F&B premises this dovetails with the existing typhoid/TY6 and food-handling course requirements in [[fnb-composite-licence]].)
- **A halal management system.** Large/medium industry must build and run a Halal Assurance System (HAS); small/micro run an Internal Halal Control System (IHCS) — both based on the MHMS 2020 manual.
- **Halal ingredients and clean premises.** All ingredients must carry a valid halal certificate, or be supported by product specs, process flow chart, and MSDS. Premises must meet hygiene standards (pest control, food-handling discipline).
- **Prayer facilities.** A prayer room or space must be provided for Muslim workers, with prayer breaks allowed.

### What disqualifies an applicant (flag these before referral)

JAKIM will not certify, among others:

- Premises serving any non-halal menu, or hotels with a kitchen preparing pork-based menus.
- Business, product, menu, or brand names that reference or resemble non-halal items — ham, bak kut teh, bacon, beer, rum, hot dog, char siew and the like.
- Products or premises with negative religious or social implications: drugs, shisha, cigarettes, hair dye, nail polish, karaoke centres, entertainment outlets.
- Unprocessed, unpackaged natural products (fresh fish, vegetables, eggs).
- Finished products merely re-labelled in Malaysia without any local processing.
- R&D-stage products not yet ready to commercialise.

If any of these apply, the client is not certifiable as-is — raise it before passing them on.

### The nine certification schemes

The MPPHM opens certification to nine schemes. Identify the right one early, because the category-specific requirements and fees differ.

1. **Food and beverage products.** Must comply with MS 1500:2019 (Halal Food — General Requirements, 3rd revision), the Food Act 1983, Food Regulations 1985, and Food Hygiene Regulations 2009. This is the most common F&B scenario alongside food premises.
2. **Food premises** (restaurant, cafe, canteen, bakery, chain food premises, mobile premises, food-court cafeteria, kiosk). Certified per premises under MS 1500:2019. Hotels are certified per kitchen/restaurant. Catering, food-service, and convention central kitchens form a sub-category.
3. **Central kitchen (dapur berpusat).** A processing and preparation facility that supplies finished or semi-finished halal food to other premises — chain outlets and mobile premises. Treated under the food-premises catering schedule. This completes the F&B picture alongside [[fnb-composite-licence]].
4. **Contract manufacturing / OEM (pengilangan kontrak).** For companies manufacturing halal products on behalf of another brand owner. Requires the contract-manufacturing agreement letter. Foreign-company contract manufacturing carries a higher fee schedule.
5. **Cosmetics products.** Each product must be notified with NPRA (or hold a manufacturing-permission letter for export-only unnotified products). Processing premises must meet the Cosmetic GMP guideline (Annex I, Part 10 of the Guidelines for Control of Cosmetic Products in Malaysia) and MS 2634:2019 (Halal Cosmetics — General Requirements), plus the Control of Drugs and Cosmetics Regulations 1984. The NPRA Notification Detail(s) document is required.
6. **Pharmaceutical products.** Each product must be registered with the Drug Control Authority (PBKD); the premises must hold an NPRA manufacturer's licence and meet GMP. Eligible categories: scheduled-poison products, over-the-counter (non-scheduled) products, health supplements, and natural/traditional complementary medicines.
7. **Consumer goods (barang gunaan).** Finished products and/or goods used in connection with food, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, or medical devices. Governed by the MS 2200 series (e.g. MS 2200-2:2013 Islamic Consumer Goods — Part 2: Usage articles).
8. **Logistics services (perkhidmatan logistik).** Covers transportation, warehousing, and retailing in the halal supply chain (integrated logistics services). Governed by the MS 2400 series: MS 2400-1:2019 (Transportation), MS 2400-2:2019 (Warehousing), MS 2400-3:2019 (Retailing). The HQC checklist asks logistics applicants for their MS 2400 documentation — risk management plan, halal policy, halal committee, and SOPs.
9. **Slaughterhouse (rumah sembelihan).** Requires a Veterinary Health Certificate from the Department of Veterinary Services (JPV) and an appointment letter from the State Islamic Religious Department for the slaughtermen in the production area.

A tenth scheme, **medical device products**, also exists — each device needs a valid Medical Device Registration Certificate from the MDA (Medical Device Authority) under the Medical Device Act 2012 (Act 737), or an equivalent supporting letter.

### Documentation checklist (HQC, domestic application)

The partner will collect these; the consultant can pre-warn the client. Core documents apply to most schemes:

- Company registration (SSM Form 9 & 49); PBT business licence; FoSIM food-premises registration certificate.
- Company yearly income statement (P&L) — used to band the industry size.
- ICs of two local Muslim staff with offer letters.
- Valid halal certificates for ingredients, or product specs + process flow chart + MSDS.
- Product process flow chart, packaging/artwork, and a map of the premises/factory location.
- For product applications: list of products, brand names, manufacturer names. For premises/catering: the menu list.
- HAS / IHCS documentation.

Scheme-specific additions: OEM contract letter (OEM); NPRA manufacturing/import licence and product registration MAL number (pharmaceutical); NPRA Notification NOT number (cosmetic); MS 2400 halal documentation (logistics); JPV veterinary certificate and slaughterman appointment letter (slaughterhouse).

Held for the JAKIM inspection day: halal awareness course certificates, staff attendance records, invoices/delivery orders, typhoid vaccination and food-handling certificates, pest-control records, batch manufacturing records, production records, and the floor plan.

### Fees (JAKIM, borne by the client)

JAKIM's processing fee is set by category and industry size, and is separate from the partner's service fee:

- **Products and services**, per category per year, banded by annual turnover: RM100 (smaller), RM400, RM700, up to RM1,000 for large industry (turnover exceeding RM50 million). Categories: food & beverage, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, consumer goods, logistics, contract/OEM, medical devices, and central kitchen.
- **Food premises:** RM100 per premises per year (restaurant/cafe, canteen, bakery, chain premises, mobile premises, food-court cafeteria, kiosk).
- **Hotel kitchens:** RM500 per kitchen/restaurant per year for 4-star and above; RM200 for 3-star and below.
- **Catering / food service / convention central kitchen:** RM100 (turnover under RM500k), RM400 (RM500k–RM5m), RM700 (over RM5m) per year.
- **Menu addition:** RM200 for a two-year period.
- **Foreign-company contract manufacturing:** RM2,500 per application (ASEAN), RM10,000 (other countries).

All figures are the JAKIM fee only; the partner's service fee (e.g. HQC's RM7,000 product package) and SST are additional.
