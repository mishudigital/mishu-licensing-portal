# Liquor licence (Lesen Minuman Keras)

**Status:** established
**Last updated:** 2026-06-19
**Sources:** `RAW/Liquor License/SOP/DBKL_GARIS PANDUAN PERMOHONAN LESEN MINUMAN KERAS.pdf`, `RAW/Liquor License/SOP/FAQ_JPPP_LMK_MY.pdf`, `RAW/Liquor License/SOP/MISHU Business License SOP Template_LIQUOR LICENSE.docx`, `RAW/Liquor License/SOP/3 – 6 Months Lead Time.docx`, `RAW/Liquor License/QUOTATION/MISHU Business License Services - Composite & Liquor (DBKL).docx`

## Summary

The liquor licence (Lesen Minuman Keras, LMK) authorises a business to sell intoxicating liquor — retail, wholesale, or for consumption on premises at a restaurant, pub, bar, or café. It is issued under the Excise Act 1976 (Akta Eksais 1976) by the District Excise Licensing Board (Lembaga Pelesenan Eksais Daerah). The defining features of this licence are strict eligibility (applicant must be non-Muslim and at least 21), a mandatory public-notice process (newspaper advertisements in four languages plus an A0 display at the premises), and compulsory Customs and Police screening. Detail below reflects the DBKL (Kuala Lumpur) process; other councils follow the same Excise Act framework with local variations.

## Body

### Eligibility

- The applicant must be **non-Muslim** and **aged 21 or above** on the application date.
- The applicant's name must appear in the SSM records, and must be one of: sole proprietor, a main partner, or a director of the relevant company.
- For the business itself: sole proprietors and partnerships must be non-Muslim owned; companies must have a **majority non-Muslim board of directors**.
- No paid-up capital is required, and any business entity can apply.

### Where liquor sales are allowed

- Permitted building types: hotels, commercial complexes, and shop-houses (rumah kedai).
- The premises must be at least **100 metres from residential areas**, and must not face a police station, place of worship, school, or hospital.
- On-premises serving (Rumah Awam) is allowed only at restaurants, eateries, pubs, bars, lounges, or club houses.
- Wholesale is allowed only at premises operating as a store/warehouse, in industrial areas/zones designated by the local authority and the Excise Act 1976.
- Retail is allowed only at supermarkets/hypermarkets. **Since 1 October 2021, no new liquor licences are issued to convenience stores, sundry shops, or Chinese medicine shops.**
- Liquor and drug stores under flats, apartments, or condominiums are not allowed; traditional-medicine and herbal shops under such buildings may not sell liquor.
- Temporary licences are issued for serving liquor at a specific event or activity.

### Application requirements (DBKL)

1. One copy of the business premises licence (the council business licence — see [[fnb-composite-licence]] for the composite that includes it).
2. One copy of the applicant's IC (21+).
3. One copy of SSM documents (Business Registration Certificate or M&A, plus Form 24 and Form 49).
4. Four passport-sized photographs of the applicant.
5. **Twelve newspaper-advertisement cuttings** of the Notis Permohonan Lesen Menjual Minuman Keras, published across **four local dailies** — one Malay, one English, one Chinese, one Tamil. Published three consecutive days, in the prescribed format. (The DBKL guideline states ad size 5cm x 9cm / 5cm x 3 columns; the FAQ states size A8, 5.2cm x 7.4cm — see Open Questions.)
6. **An A0-sized public notice** displayed at the front of the premises for seven consecutive days, in the prescribed format, stamped and confirmed by the Licensing Department (JPPP) in permanent ink before display, and monitored by JPPP.
7. Four premises photographs showing exterior, interior (different angles), and the liquor display area.
8. One location plan showing the proposed business premises (Google Maps source accepted).

### Process and timing

- Submission is **over the counter** only, and copies also go to the Customs Department and the Police Department.
- The applicant **must attend Customs and Police interviews/screening** when required, or within 14 days of submission to the central licence counter.
- New applications, transfers of ownership, and transfers of location must be submitted **no later than four weeks before** the Excise Licensing Board meeting; renewals no later than four weeks before expiry.
- **Estimated lead time: 3–6 months**, excluding the premises-licence lead time.

### Operating hours (by sale type)

| Sale type | Permitted hours |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Rumah Awam (pub / bar / lounge / restaurant) | 10:00am–12:00 midnight. Extension may be applied for in approved entertainment zones, up to a maximum of 2:00am. |
| Wholesale (borong) | 7:00am–9:00pm |
| Retail (runcit) | 7:00am–9:00pm |
| Temporary licence | As approved |

### Prohibitions

- No patron under 21 may drink liquor; a notice to this effect must be displayed at the entrance.
- No Muslim patron may drink liquor; a notice to this effect must be displayed at the entrance.
- Selling liquor in sachet form is prohibited.
- The manufacture, sale, and consumption of samsu is banned in the Federal Territory of Kuala Lumpur (effective 15 December 2020).

### Government licence fees (DBKL)

| Sale type | Fee |
|-----------|-----|
| Rumah Awam | RM110.00 per month |
| Wholesale | RM45.00 per month |
| Retail | RM35.00 per month |
| Temporary licence | RM30.00 per day |

These are government fees borne by the client, separate from MISHU's professional fee.

### Services (sales scoping reference)

A composite + liquor engagement (the worked DBKL example) breaks into these service lines: the composite licence application (paperwork, government-department checking, licence delivery), DBP signboard approval, and for the liquor licence — paperwork and approval-letter follow-up with the **Customs Department, Police (Balai Polis), Health Department, and Licensing Department**, plus the four-language newspaper advertisement and the Fire and Rescue Department. Professional fees exclude SST and agency/licence charges, which the client bears.
