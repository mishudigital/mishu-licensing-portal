# MBSA composite requirements (Shah Alam)

**Status:** emerging
**Last updated:** 2026-06-21
**Sources:** `RAW/Forms/Majlis Bandaraya Shah Alam/Senarai semak MBSA.pdf`

## Summary

Majlis Bandaraya Shah Alam (MBSA) issues a composite premises-and-signboard licence and asks for documents in four copies, organised into three parts: general documents (Bahagian A), supporting approvals from outside agencies where relevant (Bahagian B), and the signboard set (Bahagian C). MBSA's checklist is unusually explicit about renovation permits — any partition or awning needs a separate Temporary Construction Permit handled at Wisma MBSA — and about the signboard, where the activity must appear in Malay at a font 5% larger than the company name. MBSA states that a front-of-premises signboard application can be processed and approved within one hour when the Bahagian C set is complete.

## Body

### Bahagian A — general documents (4 copies)

The general set is: the completed composite licence application form; the current assessment tax (cukai taksiran) receipt for the premises; an IC copy for citizens or passport-and-trade-visa details for non-citizens (the copy crossed through and marked "Urusan MBSA sahaja"); one passport-sized photo; full SSM company documents (Form 9, 49, 24 and the MAA, plus Form 13 for any name change) or full business documents (Form D, business info, owner info) as a CTC copy printed through the MyData system with QR code and SSM logo, or an association/club registration certificate; the CF or CCC; the tenancy or sale-and-purchase agreement or land grant for private land; a location plan; a floor plan; current premises photos (front, rear, interior, surrounding) with at least one colour copy; and the signboard visual with measurements in metres plus a photo of the proposed location and the DBP language-approval letter.

### Renovation permits (Bahagian A item 7)

MBSA singles out renovation. If the premises has a partition or amendment, the applicant must supply the Temporary Construction Permit approval letter. For a shop office or rumah kedai, the partition wall and awning need a Temporary Construction Permit using the Standard Plan, arranged at the Building Department counter on Floor 8 of Wisma MBSA. For a terrace, semi-detached, or detached factory, an additional plan goes to the One Stop Centre (OSC) on the LG floor of Wisma MBSA for partition, shed, or space additions to the front, side, or rear. If there is no amendment, the applicant writes "TB / Tidak Berkaitan".

### Bahagian B — outside-agency support (4 copies, where relevant)

Supporting approvals are required only when the activity calls for them: Department of Environment (JAS) for processing or storing hazardous materials; BOMBA for flammable or dangerous materials; KDN and Police for firearms; Police review for cyber café, used-goods (barang lusuh), or used-car activity; District Office liquor licence for selling or storing alcohol; FINAS or KPDNKK for video/VCD-DVD manufacture, storage, distribution or sale; landowner permission for activity on open land; Veterinary or Perhilitan approval for animal rearing; a document bundle for waste-collection industry (lorry grant, lorry photo, collection appointment letter, dumping location, payment receipt); JKM registration for a childcare centre (taska/pusat jagaan); State Education Department approval for a kindergarten, teaching centre or tuition centre; current Town Planning (KM) approval for tadika/taska/pusat jagaan; a worker name list with IC/passport/visa for food premises; and the food-handling card (TY2 injection plus food-handling course) for food premises.

### Bahagian C — signboard (processed within 1 hour when complete)

The signboard set requires the composite form, a copy of the MBSA composite licence, and a copy of the ROB (Form D) or ROC (Form 9 & 49, or 13) or society/clinic-doctor certificate. The activity must be stated in Malay, in a font 5% larger than the company name, and positioned above it. The sketch must show the activity, the SSM-registered company name, the company address, the advertisement size, the word size, and whether the sign is lit or unlit. DBP language confirmation is mandatory (call DBP or use dbpsahbahasa.my). The signboard must not block a tiered or glass structure, must not exceed the building roof, and the structure needs sign-off from a licensed or registered engineer; a large sign also needs a BOMBA review. Only one signboard unit is allowed. A two-face sign at the side of a building needs a neighbour consent letter (Borang Kebenaran Jiran). New or additional ads involving a clinic need a Malaysian Medical Council letter, and a tourism agency needs MOTAC approval.
