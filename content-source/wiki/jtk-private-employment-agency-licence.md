## Summary

A JTK licence is what a recruitment agency needs to operate legally in Malaysia — a Private Employment Agency licence (Lesen Agensi Pekerjaan Swasta, APS) under the Private Employment Agencies Act 1981 (amended 2017), issued by the Labour Department (Jabatan Tenaga Kerja, JTK) under the Ministry of Human Resources. It comes in three types — A, B, and C — and the type sets both the scope (local placement only, versus overseas and foreign-worker placement) and the money: paid-up capital and a refundable money guarantee that rise sharply from Type A to Type C. The scoping job is to match the client's intended placements to the right type, then check the two requirements that trip applicants up most: a local individual must hold at least 51%, and the business address must be identical across every document.

## Body

### What the JTK licence is

The licence authorises a company to carry on business as a private employment agency — recruiting and placing workers (local or foreign) with another employer only. It is granted under the Private Employment Agencies Act 1981 (Akta Agensi Pekerjaan Swasta 1981, as amended in 2017), administered by JTK, with the licence approved by the Director General of Labour (Ketua Pengarah Tenaga Kerja). Applications go to the nearest Labour Office (Pejabat Tenaga Kerja) branch covering the agency's premises.

### The three licence types

The type is driven by where and whom the agency places:

- **Type A — local placement only.** Job placement for a job seeker within Malaysia. Paid-up capital RM50,000; money guarantee RM5,000.
- **Type B — overseas + foreign domestic workers.** Placement within and outside Malaysia, plus foreign domestic workers within Malaysia. Paid-up capital RM100,000; money guarantee RM100,000.
- **Type C — overseas + foreign employees.** Placement within and outside Malaysia, plus non-citizen employees within Malaysia. Paid-up capital RM250,000; money guarantee RM250,000.

The money guarantee is refundable and is paid either by bank draft or by bank guarantee (a template is held in RAW). Two practical rules on the money matter: the paid-up capital must be kept in the company's bank account (not spent down), and the money guarantee must be funded separately — it cannot be drawn from the paid-up capital. For MISHU's immigration clients, Type C (foreign employees) and Type B (foreign domestic workers) are the relevant ones; a purely local recruiter needs only Type A.

### Core requirements

- **Business description:** the company carries on business as a private employment agency to recruit and place workers to another employer only.
- **Company name:** must begin with "AGENSI PEKERJAAN" followed by the company name.
- **Shareholding:** a local individual must hold at least 51%. This is a hard floor — a fully foreign-owned recruitment agency is not licensable.
- **Premises:** must be a closed, private room with at least a table, a chair, and a bookshelf — not a shared open-plan or co-working space.
- **Business address consistency:** the address must tally across the bank statement, the local-authority (PBT) business licence, the SSM documents, and the tenancy agreement (with LHDN stamping).

### Documents (new application checklist, Form 1)

- Application form (Borang 1).
- Latest SSM company information (via SSM e-Info / MyData).
- Latest bank statement in the employment agency's name.
- Copy of the local-authority (PBT) licence, or a temporary/conditional approval letter from the PBT, in the agency's name.
- Shareholder details: IC/passport for an individual shareholder; latest SSM info / annual return for a corporate shareholder.
- Director details: IC/passport.
- Responsible director: IC plus a non-bankruptcy statement from the Malaysian Department of Insolvency (Jabatan Insolvensi).
- A statutory declaration (surat akuan bersumpah) before a commissioner for oaths.
- Processing fee RM300, by postal order or bank draft payable to "Ketua Pengarah Tenaga Kerja".

After approval, the licence fee and the money guarantee (both set by the licence type) are paid by bank draft to the same payee.

### Team-logged nuance — the address must match exactly

The single most common cause of friction is the business address. JTK places very high importance on the premises address matching across systems line by line: the address on the SSM record, the PBT licence, and Borang 1 must be identical. The official checklist states this outright ("ensure the business address on the SSM, PBT licence and Borang 1 are the same"). Before submitting, verify the address matches the local council's system exactly — a mismatch, even a formatting difference, holds up the application.

### Fees and timeline (MISHU)

On a worked MBPJ quotation for a company setting up as an agency (office premises), MISHU's service fee for the JTK licence (Type C / overseas + foreign employees) was RM3,000–RM6,000 (final figure confirmed with the business licence department), with a roughly 3-month timeline. That sat alongside the composite premises licence (RM850) and the fire certificate (RM600) for a total service fee of RM6,450. The government charges — RM300 processing fee, the licence fee, and the money guarantee — are separate and borne by the client.
